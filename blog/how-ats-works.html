How ATS Systems Kill 75% of Resumes (And How KINETK Beats Them)

By KINETK Team March 24, 2026 5 min read

What Is ATS and Why It Matters

Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) are software platforms used by recruiters and HR departments to filter, rank, and manage job applications. Almost every mid-to-large company uses one. They're designed to save time by automatically screening thousands of resumes against job requirements.

The problem? These systems aren't designed with job seekers in mind. They're optimized for corporate efficiency—which means if your resume doesn't match their criteria, it gets rejected automatically, often without a human ever seeing it.

The statistics are sobering: 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS systems before a human recruiter ever looks at them. For competitive roles, your resume has to pass the machine before it can impress the person.

The 6 Most Common Ways Resumes Fail ATS

Not all resumes fail ATS equally. Here are the most common culprits:

1. Keyword Gaps

ATS systems scan for specific keywords from the job description. If your resume doesn't contain those exact terms, it's ranked lower or rejected outright. This is the #1 reason resumes fail—even strong candidates can be filtered out simply because they used synonyms or different terminology.

2. Formatting Issues (Tables, Columns, Graphics)

Many modern resume templates use two-column layouts, text boxes, or sidebar graphics to look professional. ATS systems can't parse these. They'll either strip the formatting and scramble your content, or fail to read certain sections entirely.

3. Non-Standard Section Headings

ATS looks for standard headers like "Experience," "Education," and "Skills." If you use creative titles like "Impact" or "Achievements," the system may not recognize these sections and skip them.

4. Wrong File Format

PDFs can cause parsing errors. Fancy Word formatting with embedded objects can be unreadable. ATS prefers simple .docx files or plain text. Some systems flat-out reject PDFs or images embedded in resumes.

5. Incorrect Job Title Capitalization or Spelling

If the job posting says "Software Engineer" and your resume says "software engineer" or "Softare Engineer," ATS may fail to match it. Consistency matters—a lot.

6. Dates in Non-Standard Formats

Use "January 2024" or "01/2024" formats. Non-standard date formats confuse ATS parsers and can cause section skipping.
